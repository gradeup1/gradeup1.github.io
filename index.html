Snakes

Pythons

Triangular heads, covered with small scales and have pits on their lips. Have a muscular body and active in the early morning or evening.

Typical Snakes

Head is covered with large, symmetrical scales on the crown; Belly scales enlarged. Body form, colouration, and habitats vary.

Cobras and Mambas

Large front fangs, body form, colouration, and habitats very varied. Except for garter snakes, all threaten by raising the forebody and inflating or spreading the neck.

Burrowing Primitive Snakes

Head blunt, with vestigial eyes; body shiny, and tail short; belly scales not enlarged. Usually plain black or brown in color.Burrow underground; wriggle but never bite.

Adders

Head triangular, covered with small scales(excep night adders), and usually with a prominent mark (V-shapes or arrow-shaped) on the crown; body fat, with rough, small scales on top, tail very short. They give warning with a hiss and strike readily.
