Eastern Green Mamba

(Dendroaspis angusticeps)

Description

Commonly called the Green Mamba, the snake Dendroaspis angusticeps is a long, slender arboreal snake native to much of East Africa. This speaies belongs to the family Elapidae, which includes cobras, Coral snakes, kraits, and sea snakes. The genus Dendroaspis also contains the Jameson's mamba(D.Jameson), the West African green mamba(D.viridis), and the Black mamba(D.polylepis).
They are the smallest of the species and reach an average of 1.5m in length.


Classification

Extremely Venomous


Reproduction

Oviparous(egg laying)
They can lay between 6 and 18 eggs in the summer. Hatchlings are Venomous from birth.


Distribution

East Africa


Diet

Birds, bird eggs, and small mammals

Mole Snake

(Pseudapis cana)

Description

Highly successful snake that come in a wide variety of colors from pitch black on the West coast, to reddish brown, grey, light orange, ashy grey and salmon in some areas. Juveniles are heavily patterned but these patterns typically fade with age. It is a powerful constrictor and can be aggressive, will not hesitate to bite when handled. They can reach up to 2m in length.


Classification

Harmless(Adults can inflict a bad bite)


Reproduction


Distribution

Most common in the Western Cape, Free State and Mpumalanga.


Diet

They feed largely on rodents which are actively sought after in their burrows but birds and lizards are readily taken.

Common Slug-Eater

(Duberria lutrix)

Description

Adults can be up to 30 to 40 cm.
The common Slug-Eater's colour can vary, but they typically have an olive green to Burrowing or russet back,grey flanks, a yellow or cream belly, and a black, more or less complete, vertical stripe.


Classification

Non-venomous


Reproduction

Ovoviviparous


Distribution

South Africa, Mozambique, Eswatini, Ethiopia, Kenya, Uganda, Zimbabwe, Tanzania, Rwanda, DRC, and Burundi


Diet

As the name implies, the common slug eater is a specialised predator and feeds on snails and slugs, mostly finding its prey through chemoreception, using its tongue. It swallows its prey quickly before too much defensive mucus is produced, extracting snails from their shells through the shell opening, or by smashing the shell against a rock while grasping the soft body in its jaws.

Brown House Snake

(Boaedon capensis)

Description

One of the most common and widespread snakes in South Africa. Easily distinguishable by the two white lines running down the side of the head.


Classification

Harmless


Reproduction

Distribution

These snakes can be found in almost every environment from surburban gardens, forests, grasslands, and including the drier arid regions of the Karoo.

Diet

These snakes feed mainly on rats, mice, lizards, birds, and even bats.

Rhombic Egg Eater

(Dasypeltis scabra)

Description

These snakes average around 50-60cm but can reach over 1m in certain areas large enough to consume an adult chicken egg.


Classification

Harmless


Reproduction

Distribution

Just like the Brown House Snake, the Rhombic Egg Eater can be found just about anywhere in South Africa.
From the arid regions in the West, the coastal forests in the Eatern half of the country, grasslands and montane fynbos.


Diet

Feed exclusively on birds eggs and can go many months without a meal when the birding season comes to an end.
